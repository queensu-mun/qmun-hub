# Conference Dossier: UPMUNC (University of Pennsylvania Model UN Conference)

Strategy brief for UPMUNC in Philadelphia: format mix, what its chairs reward, weekend pacing, and how Queen's converts. Our 2024 DISEC verbal commendation is the proof of concept.

## The basics

UPMUNC is run by Penn's International Affairs Association and is one of the longest-running collegiate conferences anywhere, now approaching its 59th edition. It runs in mid-October in downtown Philadelphia at a conference hotel, draws around 700 delegates, and is staffed by over 300 Penn students under a seventeen-member secretariat. That staffing depth matters: rooms are well-run, crisis backrooms are responsive, and chairs are usually experienced circuit competitors themselves, which raises the bar for what impresses them.

Unlike NCSC, UPMUNC is a full-spectrum conference. It runs General Assembly simulations, ECOSOC and specialized bodies, and a large crisis wing. This is the conference where a balanced delegation gets rewarded, and where our GA specialists get their best shot at hardware on the fall circuit.

## What UPMUNC rewards

UPMUNC sits early in the fall season and attracts the heaviest hitters on the circuit. UChicago won Best Large Delegation there in the 2024-25 season, and Georgetown, American, and the other top-ten programs all show up at strength. Treat the room quality as playoff level: an Honorable Mention at UPMUNC is worth more developmentally than a gavel at a soft regional.

In GA rooms like DISEC, where we earned our 2024 verbal commendation, the formula at this level is consistency across all four days rather than one great speech. Chairs at deep conferences are tracking who drove the working paper that survived the merge, who actually wrote the operative clauses everyone cites, and who held a bloc together when two papers competed for the same co-sponsors. A 100-plus person GA room at UPMUNC will produce maybe 8 to 10 awards. The math says you must be one of the three or four names the dais writes down every single session.

In crisis and specialized rooms, the standard is the circuit standard: tight directive writing, a coherent personal arc, and frontroom presence that does not abandon the committee topic. Penn backrooms are well staffed, so directive volume gets processed quickly; quality and arc coherence separate delegates, not raw output.

## Pacing of the weekend

UPMUNC runs Thursday evening through Sunday midday. The rhythm:

- Thursday night: opening speeches and first bloc formation. In a big GA room, the delegates who exit Thursday with five committed allies and a named bloc have already banked half the weekend.
- Friday: two to three long sessions. Working papers get drafted and the merge wars begin. This is the highest-leverage day for GA delegates.
- Saturday: draft resolutions finalize, amendments fly, crisis arcs peak. Saturday night is the delegate social; go, because bloc relationships survive into Sunday voting.
- Sunday: voting procedure and closing. GA awards are frequently decided by who manages the chaos of voting bloc defections gracefully.

Philadelphia logistics are easy from Kingston compared to deeper US trips: it is a manageable drive or a short flight, which makes UPMUNC one of the most cost-efficient elite reps on our calendar.

## What strong delegations do

- They assign GA rooms to people who genuinely like GA. At a conference with real crisis options, delegates dumped into DISEC against their will underperform. Our verbal commendation came from a delegate who wanted that room.
- They pre-write position framing, not speeches. Top chairs can tell a memorized speech from a delegate actually responding to the debate.
- They scout the merge early. By Friday afternoon there are usually two or three viable papers in a big GA room. Strong delegates pick the winning merge a session before it happens, or force it on their own terms.
- They use head delegates as floaters. The best programs have non-competing leaders walking between rooms, carrying intelligence about which blocs are forming and coaching at breaks.

## Queen's plan, concretely

UPMUNC should be our GA showcase plus a crisis development trip. Put our two most experienced GA delegates in the large assemblies with explicit award mandates, and use the crisis wing to give second-year delegates reps against elite competition without the pressure of carrying the team score.

Prep: country and topic research finished ten days out, one full mock GA session running the actual UPMUNC topic with the actual country assignments, and a working paper drafting drill under 45-minute time pressure. Set the bar honestly: converting the 2024 verbal commendation into an Honorable Mention or Outstanding in a GA room at UPMUNC would be a real step up the circuit ladder, and stacking two or three individual awards puts us in the Small Delegation conversation.
