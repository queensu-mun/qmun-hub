# Conference Dossier: McMUN (McGill Model United Nations)

Strategy brief for McMUN in Montreal: the biggest stage in Canada, what its rooms reward, weekend rhythm, and why this is the conference Queen's should target hardest. Our archive already holds McMUN working papers from 2025 and 2026.

## The basics

McMUN is hosted by McGill through IRSAM in late January in Montreal. It is the largest collegiate Model UN conference in Canada and the second largest in North America, drawing over 1,000 delegates with a staff of around 250. Delegates voted it the best conference on the circuit in the 2024-25 season, and the top American programs treat it as a mandatory stop: it is one of the marquee conferences a team must perform at to crack the Best Delegate top rankings.

The committee menu is broad and the sizes are published: General Assemblies run up to 200 delegates, specialized agencies run 30 to 50, and crisis committees run 10 to 30. Recent editions have included traditional UN bodies, a UNSC simulation, an Ad Hoc Specialized Agency, an ICAO committee, cabinets, and boards of directors. McMUN is comfortable with creative and non-traditional premises in a way NCSC is not, so the prep has to flex.

## Why McMUN matters most for Queen's

Three reasons. First, proximity: Montreal is a three-hour drive from Kingston, which means we can send our largest delegation of the year at the lowest cost per delegate. Second, depth plus breadth: novices can get reps in 200-person GAs while our award candidates hunt in specialized and crisis rooms, all on one trip. Third, it is our rivalry conference. McGill is the strongest Canadian program on the circuit, and beating McGill delegates in McGill's own building is the clearest possible signal that the Queen's program is ascending. Western, Toronto, and the rest of the Canadian schools are all in the room too.

We already have institutional knowledge here: the SLAP Sierra Leone UNSC working paper from McMUN 2026 and the FARM to Table WFP paper from McMUN 2025 live in the archive. Both show the house style that works at this conference: comprehensive papers, 25 to 30 operative clauses, a coherent naming theme, and broad co-sponsorship.

## What McMUN rewards

In the giant GAs, visibility is the currency and it is brutally scarce. With up to 200 delegates and a speakers list measured in hours, the award path runs through unmods and paper leadership, not the formal list. The dais in a room that size can only track a dozen names; you must be one of them by the end of the first evening. Working paper architecture matters enormously: the delegate who structures the dominant paper and manages a 20-country co-sponsor bloc is the one who gavels.

In specialized agencies of 30 to 50, McMUN rewards delegates who balance substance with the creative premise. These rooms are the sweet spot for our experienced delegates: small enough for the dais to know everyone, large enough that winning one is impressive.

In crisis rooms of 10 to 30, the standard circuit crisis game applies, with one McMUN twist: directors enjoy inventive arcs and the conference culture tolerates more flair than NCSC's realism norm. Creativity is rewarded if it stays internally consistent.

## Pacing of the weekend

McMUN runs Thursday evening to Sunday. Montreal in January adds real logistics: winter driving, coat check lines, and a famously intense social calendar. McParté is a genuine circuit institution, and the practical coaching point is simple: the social schedule is a test of discipline. Delegates who manage it show up sharp for the long Saturday sessions while half their competition fades. Friday is paper-building day in every format. Saturday is merge day and crisis climax day. Sunday morning sessions are short but awards are frequently settled by Sunday composure during voting.

## What strong delegations do

- They bring numbers with structure. A 20-plus person delegation only converts if every delegate has a named coach contact, a room-specific goal, and a check-in cadence. Big teams without structure dissolve into tourism.
- They put paper-writers in GAs. McMUN GA awards follow the working paper, so seed our best drafters there and have them recruit co-sponsors relentlessly from hour one.
- They prepare for creative premises. Read the background guide twice and take the premise seriously, even when it is unconventional. Delegates who fight the premise lose the room.
- They scout. With every Canadian rival plus the American top ten present, McMUN is the best scouting weekend of the year. Head delegates should log which schools placed where and how their styles read.

## Queen's plan, concretely

Send the full travel team. Award candidates go to specialized agencies and crisis; second-years take GA seats with explicit paper-leadership mandates; novices observe-compete with a defined skills checklist. Run a pre-McMUN mock in early January using a past McMUN background guide, and review the SLAP and FARM papers as templates in training. Goals: multiple individual awards, at least one gavel attempt in a specialized room, and a complete scouting log on McGill, Toronto, and Western.
