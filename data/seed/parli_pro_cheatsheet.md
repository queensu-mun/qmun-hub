# Parliamentary Procedure Cheat Sheet

## Points (interrupt floor without vote)

| Point | When | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Personal Privilege** | Anytime, even mid-speech | Address physical comfort (hear better, leave room) |
| **Order** | If chair or delegate violates procedure | Chair rules immediately |
| **Inquiry** | When floor is open (not during speech) | Ask chair a procedural question |
| **Information** | After a delegate speech, with permission | Ask the speaker a substantive question |
| **Parliamentary Inquiry** | Anytime | Clarify a rule with the chair |

## Motions (require recognition; many require vote)

### Procedural motions
- **Open debate** (start of session): simple majority
- **Adjourn debate**: simple majority, ends session permanently for this topic
- **Suspend debate**: simple majority, ends today's session
- **Set speakers list time / individual speaking time**: simple majority
- **Close debate / Move to voting procedure**: 2/3 majority
- **Reorder draft resolutions for voting**: simple majority
- **Reconsider a resolution that just failed**: 2/3 majority

### Caucus motions
- **Moderated caucus** (specify topic + total time + speaking time): simple majority
- **Unmoderated caucus** (specify total time): simple majority
- **Round-robin** (each delegate speaks in order): simple majority

### Voting motions
- **Roll call vote** (instead of placard vote): one delegate's request honored
- **Divide the question**: separate operative clauses for individual votes; simple majority
- **Pass / Yes with rights / No with rights / Abstain**: choices on substantive vote with right of reply

## Yields (after a speech, with leftover time)

After your speech, you can yield your remaining time:
- **Yield to another delegate** (must be off speakers list): they get your remaining time
- **Yield to questions**: floor takes points of information
- **Yield to chair**: time forfeited

You **cannot** yield to a delegate who is on the speakers list or has already spoken in this round.

## Voting

| Vote type | Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Procedural | Simple majority | Cannot abstain |
| Substantive (most resolutions) | Simple majority | Can abstain; rights of reply available |
| Substantive (UNSC P5 veto matters) | 9 of 15 + no P5 veto | Specific to Security Council simulations |
| Constitutional (rare) | 2/3 majority | Used for major rule changes |

## Speakers list

- **General Speakers List (GSL)** runs throughout debate; new speakers added when chair calls
- A delegate cannot be added twice in a row (must wait one cycle)
- Speakers list can be motion-extended at end
- After moderated caucus on subtopic, GSL resumes

## Right of Reply

If a delegate speech makes statements that personally insult or misrepresent your country, you can request a Right of Reply from the chair. Rare; chairs grant sparingly. The replying delegate gets a short defined time (usually 30s-2min).

## Common chair phrases

- "The floor is open" — caucus or speakers list active
- "Are there any points or motions on the floor?" — chair fishing for delegate-driven movement
- "Seeing none, the speaker has the floor" — proceed
- "The speaker's time has elapsed" — gentle stop
- "Will the delegate yield?" — chair asks if you'll allow questions
- "Is the delegation in order?" — chair asks if you can vote

## Style notes (NAIMUN-style vs THIMUN-style)

- **NAIMUN-style** (most US conferences): formal speakers list, working papers become draft resolutions through formal process, more procedural emphasis
- **THIMUN-style** (some European + select US): rolling speakers list, working papers more fluid, lobbying time more emphasized
- **Crisis committees**: less procedure, more directives and notes

Most North American collegiate conferences are NAIMUN-style. Confirm in the rules of procedure document the conference sends.
