\#\# Queen's Team Culture & Preparation

**\- How does the team prepare before a conference? What's the timeline? (e.g., 4 weeks out: assign committees, 2 weeks out: draft position papers, 1 week out: mock debate?)**

* Usually conference acceptance is sent out \~3 weeks to a month before the conference takes place. Background guides are usually released by the conference in or around that time. Depending on committee type research varies.   
  - Double delegation committees must take more type to prepare with each other and get "political" strategy sorted out   
  - ADHOC committees are virtually no preparation  
  - In my experience GA committees require more research prior to conferences than crisis committees do   
    * Crisis committees you will want to plan out a rough map of an arc    
      * Careful not to plan out too much of it though as if things do not go exactly to plan you will struggle (this happened to Jake and I at NCSC UNSC in fall of 2025\)  
* Position papers should be started about a week to 10 days out from conference but will dive into that more later   
* Aside from our weekly meetings and now soon to be twice a week meetings we do not really meet as a group (the smaller group) going to conferences before we leave but perhaps we should   
  - Just as simple as getting a drink and getting to know each other a bit more before we leave could be good for social cohesion of a team   
  - This also allows more experienced delegates going to help workshop strategies for the newer delegates attending the conference. 

**\- How do you divide research responsibilities within a delegation?**

* For a conference everybody does their own research and handles their own committees   
* For our mock meetings on a twice a week basis we have a bank of past conference background guides that we pick form to inform our debate topics that week   
* I have a 3 question research guide that I use to quickly and effectively research any topic (mostly applicable to GA/SA but can also be applicable to crisis)  
  - Question 1: How does your country / character feel about this issue   
    * What domestic actions have been taken?  
  - Question 2: How does the international community / other members of community feel about this issue  
    * What past international actions have been taken?  
  - What are the next steps of this issue?  
    * What is our strategy going into committee   
    * How do you want to solve this issue?  
  - I also use this method to write my position papers in 3 paragraphs   
* We do need more of a mentorship program for our newer delegates on how to research   
  - This is exponential for competitive delegates who can benefit from wisdom on how to actually prepare for a first conference

**\- Do you do practice sessions or mock debates? What do those look like?**

* Historically our team has had 1, 2 hour meeting a week on Monday nights, recently after a bad MCMUN performance and a push from some of our more competitive delegates on the team (including myself (Jack)) we are going to now have a second meeting a week on Thursdays  
* The plan from now on is to have a more lighthearted mock on Monday nights and have more specialized intense trainings / mocks on Thursdays for our more competitive delegates   
  - This will lets us keep the fun atmosphere of the team (integral to our identity and success) while also striving to be better   
* Typically the monday meeting topic is sent out hours before the meeting (I want this to be longer) and it is a topic with a background guide form our bank of them   
  - It allows for people to sign up and we try to have a variety of both GA and Crisis committees although it is tough to host a crisis mock because we lack the staff to do a comprehensive backroom  
* The Monday meeting provides a good chance for all members to debate and polish their in room performances but lack much of a training on outroom as many mocks just progress through stages of debate and hardly reach working papers   
  - This is also in part because 2 hours is not a lot of time to get through a mock 

\- **What does a first-timer need to know that the handbook doesn't cover? Unwritten rules, social dynamics, what to pack, what to expect emotionally.**

* The Art of MUN covers a lot of the procedure of MUN and is a good introduction to the world  
  - I recently wrote up on the how to win for GA and Jake did the How to win for crisis I think those are still relevant to how we train our delegates   
* One thing that is not mentioned directly but is essentially to the social fabric of the team is going to the bar after our Monday meetings  
  - It can be intimidating to walk onto a team of socially tight individuals who are really good at public speaking   
    * It was tough for me when I first joined the team   
  - The two ways to break in socially are by showing up to the bar and by going to conferences   
    * There you are with a smaller group of people and bonding   
  - Going to the bar makes us a better team because we are closer and our friends we encourage all new members to come but we need more only about \~20 people a week join out of a 100 person team 

**\- What's the team's general philosophy — aggressive vs. collaborative, awards-focused vs. experience-focused? Has it shifted over time?**

* Generally we have a good reputation on the collegiate circuit as a team that is friendly, collaborative and easy to work with   
* We do not put a large emphasis on awards but they do certainly matter   
  - We like to give our newer hirers conference experience but we like to push to be competitive  
  - What we care about is everyone giving their best effort to award and not just be there to do nothing  
    * We know that awards will follow with this philosophy   
* Every delegate has a different style and that comes through attending meetings, our internal conferences, and external conferences on the collegiate circuit
